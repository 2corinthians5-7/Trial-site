Neurodiversity in Minors

Key points

What is Neurodiversity?

[1] Having neurodiversity makes someone’s brain function differently from the typical brain.

[2] 30-40% of people have neurodiversity, and in Hong Kong, [3] there are 10-20% of people worldwide who have neurodiversity.

There are different types of neurodiversity, for example, autism, ADHD and tourettes.

[4] People with autism may act like the following:

  • Difficulty talking and acting in front of others.
  • Troubles understanding people.
  • Constant repetition of the same actions (in a methodical manner).

[5] People with ADHD would not be able to keep focus, hyperactivity and impulsivity.

People with tourettes would have both motor and vocal tics which occur several times a day and it’s uncontrollable.

Bullying the neurominority

[5] Having a disability can make children and teens a higher chance being a target of bullies
